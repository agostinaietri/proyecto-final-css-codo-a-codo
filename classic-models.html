IBM PC

release date: 1981

It broke all kinds of preconceived notions about computers -- it was affordable, it was small, and it made the term "PC" commonplace.


Macintosh

release date: 1984

This computer was such a hit that today's Apple computers are its direct descendants, nearly 30 years later. It had a graphical user interface just like the Lisa.

Commodore 64

release date: 1982

The Commodore might just be the most famous home computer. Between 1982 and 1993, almost 30 million of them were sold around the world.



ZX Spectrum

release date: 1982

The Spectrum did for the UK what the Commodore 64 did for the US. It was produced by Clive Sinclair, who earned a knighthood for services to British industry.


Analytical machine

relase date: 1832

The Analytical Engine was a mechanical general-purpose computer designed by English mathematician and computer pioneer Charles Babbage



iMac

release date: 1998

Up until the iMac, computers were boring beige boxes. Apple revolutionized the idea of what computers could look like when they unveiled a curvy, jaw-dropping new line available in a number of explosive colors.
