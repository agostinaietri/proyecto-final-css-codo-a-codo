1801

In France, Joseph Marie Jacquard invents
a loom that uses punched wooden cards
to automatically weave fabric designs.
Early computers would use similar punch cards.



1822

1822: English mathematician Charles Babbage
conceives of a steam-driven calculating machine that would
be able to compute tables of numbers. The project, funded
by the English government, is a failure. More than a century
later, however, the world's first computer was actually built.



1936

Alan Turing presents the notion of a universal machine, later
called the Turing machine, capable of computing anything.
The central concept of the computer was based on his ideas.



1943-1944

Two University of Pennsylvania professors, John Mauchly and
J. Presper Eckert, build the Electronic Numerical Integrator and
Calculator (ENIAC). Considered the grandfather of computers,
it fills a 20-foot by 40-foot room and has 18,000 vacuum tubes.



1953

Grace Hopper develops the first computer language,
which eventually becomes known as COBOL. Thomas Johnson
Watson Jr., son of IBM CEO Thomas Johnson Watson Sr.,
conceives the IBM 701 EDPM to help the United Nations
keep tabs on Korea during the war.



1976

Steve Jobs and Steve Wozniak start Apple Computers
on April Fool's Day and roll out the Apple I, the first computer
with a single-circuit board, according to Stanford University.



1983

Apple's Lisa is the first personal computer with
a GUI. It also features a drop-down menu and icons. It flops but
eventually evolves into the Macintosh. The Gavilan SC is the first
portable computer with the familiar flip form factor and the
first to be marketed as a "laptop."



1985

Microsoft announces Windows, according
to Encyclopedia Britannica. This was the company's response
to Apple's GUI. Commodore unveils the Amiga 1000, which
features advanced audio and video capabilities.



1990

Tim Berners-Lee, a researcher at CERN, the
high-energy physics laboratory in Geneva, develops HyperText
Markup Language (HTML), giving rise to the World Wide Web



2016

The first reprogrammable quantum computer was created.
Until now, there hasn't been any quantum-computing platform
that had the capability to program new algorithms into their
system. They're usually tailored to attack a particular algorithm.
